With these and the other booty he sentNicias, pretor to the Achaeans, to ^Egium; and goingto Corinth, he ordered his army to march by landthrough Bosotia, while he himself, sailing from Cen-chrea, along the coast of Attica, round the promontoryof Sunium, reached Chalcis, after passing almostthrough the middle of the enemy's fleet. Havinghighly commended the fidelity and bravery of the in­habitants, in not suffering either fear or hope to in­fluence their minds, and having exhorted them to per­severe in maintaining the alliance with the same con­stancy, if they preferred their present situation tothat of the inhabitants of Oreum and Opus, he sailedto Oreum; and having there conferred the directionof affairs, and the command of the city, on such of thechief inhabitants as had chosen to fly rather than sur­render to the Romans, he sailed over from Euboea toDemetrias, from whence he had at first set out toassist his allies.
