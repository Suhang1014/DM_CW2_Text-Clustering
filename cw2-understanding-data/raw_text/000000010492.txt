This had beenhis earnest request, lest his head (b) shouldfall into the hands of his enemies, and bemade a public spectacle. He was borneon the shoulders of the praetorian soldiersto the funeral pile. The men, during theprocession, paid all marks of respect tohis remains. They printed kisses on hishands, and on the mortal wound, and, ina flood of tears, poured forth their warmestpraise. At the funeral pile some of thesoldiers put an end to their lives; not fromany consciousness of guilt, nor yet im­pelled by fear; but to emulate the ex­ample of their prince, and to show them­selves faithful to the last. At Bedriacum,Placentia, and other camps, numbers fol­lowed the example. A sepulchre (c) wasraised to the memory of Otho, but of anordinary structure, protected by its mean­ness, and therefore likely to last. L.
