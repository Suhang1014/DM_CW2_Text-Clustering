Toentertain him on his march, the principalmen of every city were obliged to lavishall their wealth, and the country was ex­hausted. The soldiers, degenerating intoa band of epicures, lost all regard for mi­litary duty. They despised their prince,yet followed his example. Vitellius, byan edict sent forward to Rome, signifiedhis pleasure to postpone for the presentthe title of Augustus; and for that ofCaesar, he declined, it altogether. Theprerogative of the prince was sufficientfor his ambition. He ordered the mathe­maticians to be banished out of Italy,and, under heavy penalties, restrained theRoman knights from disgracing themselvesby fighting prizes like common gladiators,and by exhibiting their persons on thepublic stage.
