By this animating speech allwho assisted at the council were inspiredwith new confidence. They pressed roundVespasian, exhorting him to undertake theenterprise; they recalled to his memorythe responses of oracles (a), and the pre­dictions of men skilled in judicial astro­logy. Nor was Vespasian untincturcdwith that superstition. Even afterwards,when possessed of the supreme authority,he retained a mathematician named Seleu-cus, to assist his councils with his insightinto future events. A number of progno­stics, that occurred to him in his youth,came fresh in his mind.
