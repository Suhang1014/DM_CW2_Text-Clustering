According to his discre­tion, a father might chastise the real or imaginaryfaults of his children, by stripes, by imprisonment,by exile, by sending them to the country to work inchains among the meanest of his servants. The ma­jesty of a parent was armed with the power of lifeand death*; and the examples of such bloody exe­cutions, which were sometimes praised and neverpunished, may be traced in the annals of Rome, be­yond the times of Pompey and Augustus.
