If we compute the number and duration of the reigns,it will be found, that a period of six hundred years isfilled by sixty emperors, including in the Augustan listsome female sovereigns; and deducting some usurperswho were never acknowledged in the capital, and someprinces who did not live to possess their inheritance.The average proportion will allow ten years for eachemperor, far below the chronological rule of Sir IsaacNewton, who, from the experience of more recent andregular monarchies, has defined about eighteen or twentyyears as the term of an ordinary reign. The Byzantineempire was most tranquil and prosperous when it couldacquiesce in hereditary succession; five dynasties, theHeraclian, Isaurian, Amorian, Basilian, and Comnenianfamilies, enjoyed and transmitted the royal patrimonyduring their respective series of five, four, three, six, andfour generations; several princes number the years oftheir reign with those of their infancy: and Constan-tine VII. and his two grandsons occupy the space of anentire century. But in the intervals of the Byzantinedynasties, the succession is rapid and broken, and thename of a successful candidate is speedily erased by amore fortunate competitor.
