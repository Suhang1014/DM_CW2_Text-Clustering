As great numbers were crouded together in thishollow dungeon, the beams of the fun, in the first place,and then the suffocating air, annoyed them in a moreterrible manner, because the aperture was left unco­vered; and each succeeding night, the reverse of thepreceding day, autumnal and nipping, through suchvicissitudes threw them into strange disorders. Thusstraitened as they were for room, they did whateverthey had to do on one and the same spot; and the car­cases of those who died lay heaped up promiscuously to­gether, as some expired of their wounds, and othersperished through the viciflitudes of air they suffered, orsome other such deadly cause.
