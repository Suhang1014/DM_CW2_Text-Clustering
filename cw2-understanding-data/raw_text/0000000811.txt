The ancient ritesof Greece were imitated by the royal colony ofAntioch. A stream of prophecy, which rivalled thetruth and reputation of the Delphic oracle, flowedfrom the Castalian fountain of Daphne. In the ad­jacent fields a stadium was built by a special privilege,which had been purchased from Elis; the Olympicgames were celebrated at the expense of the city ; anda revenue of thirty thousand pounds sterling wasannually applied to the public pleasures. The per­petual resort of pilgrims and spectators insensiblyformed, in the neighbourhood of the temple, thestately and populous village of Daphne, which emu­lated the splendour, without acquiring the title, of aprovincial city.
