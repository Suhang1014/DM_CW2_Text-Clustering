The Catholicshated the person and government of Copronymus;but even their hatred is a proof of their oppression.They dissemble the provocations which might excuseor justify his rigour, but even these provocationsmust gradually inflame his resentment, and hardenhis temper, in the use or abuse of despotism. Yetthe character of the fifth Constantine was not devoidof merit, nor did his government always deserve thecurses or contempt of the Greeks. From the con­fession of his enemies, I am informed of the restora­tion of an ancient aqueduct, of the redemption oftwo thousand five hundred captives, of the uncom­mon plenty of the times, and of the new colonies withwhich he repeopled Constantinople and the Thraciaflcities.
