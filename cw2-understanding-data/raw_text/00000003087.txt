But when the enemy sent Gorgias, with five thousandfoot, and one thousand horse, that he might fall upon Judas bynight, and had for that purpose certain of the runagate Jews asguides, the son of Mattathias perceived it, and resolved to fallupon those enemies that were in their camp, now their forceswere divided. When they had therefore supped in good time,and had left many fires in their camp he marched all night tothose enemies that were at Emmaus; so that when Gorgiasfound no enemy in their camp, but suspected that they were re­tired and had hidden themselves among the mountains, he resol­ved to go and seek them wheresoever they were.
