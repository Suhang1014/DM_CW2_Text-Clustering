The citywas full of soldiers; and among men in­flamed with liquor, no union, no con­certed measure, could be expected. Thetraitors desisted from their purpose, butwith no public motive. The general wel­fare made no impression on men, who hadconspired to imbrue their hands in theblood of their sovereign. What theychiefly feared was, that the first whooffered himself to the troops from Ger­many and Pannonia, might by thosestrangers, and in the tumult of the dark,be mistaken for Otho, and saluted by thetitle of emperor.
