If any one had a vocation to the chief command in the Asiatic war, it was Sulla. He had a few years before com­manded with the greatest success in the same theatre ofwar; he had contributed more than any other man to thesubjugation of the dangerous Italian insurrection; as consulof the year in which the Asiatic war broke out, he had beeninvested with the command in it after the customary wayand with the full consent of bis colleague, who was onfriendly terms with him and related to him by marriage.
