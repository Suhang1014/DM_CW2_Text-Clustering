Some estimate may be formedof the power of the Greek emperors, by the curiousand minute detail of the armament which was preparedfor the reduction of Crete. A fleet of one hundredand twelve galleys and seventy-five vessels of thePamphylian style was equipped in the capital, theislands of the iEgean sea, and the sea-ports of Asia,Macedonia, and Greece. It carried thirty-four thou­sand mariners, seven thousand three hundred andforty soldiers, seven hundred Russians, and five thou­sand and eighty-seven Mardaites, whose fathers hadbeen transplanted from the mountains of Libanus.Their pay, most probably of a month, was computedat thirty-four centenaries of gold, about one hundredand thirty-six thousand pounds sterling.
