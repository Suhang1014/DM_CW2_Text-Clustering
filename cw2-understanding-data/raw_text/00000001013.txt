Every calumny that couldwound the reputation of the Apostate was cre­dulously embraced by the fears and hatred of his ad­versaries; and their indiscreet clamours provokedthe temper of a sovereign, whom it was their duty torespect, and their interest to flatter. They stillprotested, that prayers and tears were their onlyweapons against the impious tyrant, whose head theydevoted to the justice of offended Heaven. But theyinsinuated, with sullen resolution, that their sub­mission was no longer the effect of weakness; andthat, in the imperfect state of human virtue, thepatience, which is founded on principle, may be ex­hausted by persecution. It is impossible to deter­mine how far the zeal of Julian would have prevailedover his good sense and humanity: but, if we se­riously reflect on the strength and spirit of the church,we shall be convinced, that, before the emperorcould have extinguished the religion of Christ, hemust have involved his country in the horrors of acivil war.
