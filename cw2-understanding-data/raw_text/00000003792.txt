IV- polis, which is distant twenty furlongs from Alexandria:there he put his army on board some long ships, and sailedupon the river along the Mendesian Nomus, as far as thocity Thmuis: there he got out of the ships, and walked onfoot, and lodged all night at a small city called Tunis. Hissecond station was Heracleopolis, and his third Pclusium ; hethen refreshed his army at that place for two days, and onthe third passed over the mouths of Nile at Pelusium; hethen proceeded one station over the desert, and pitched hiscamp at the temple of the Casian Jupiter,1 and on the nextday at Ostracine. This station had no water, but the peopleof the country make use of water brought from other places.After this he rested at Rhinocolura, and from thence he wentto Raphia, which was his fourth station. This city is thebeginning of Syria. For his fifth station he pitched his campat Gaza; after which he came to Ascalon, and thence toJamnia, and after that to Joppa, and from Joppa to Caesarea,having taken a resolution to gather all his other forces toge­ther at that place. 1 There are coins of this Casian Jupiter still extant, as Spanheim here in­forms us.
