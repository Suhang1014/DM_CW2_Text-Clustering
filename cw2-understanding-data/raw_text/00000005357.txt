At length, on a singular bridge, constructed withart and difficulty, of large hogsheads, he transported his army tothe opposite bank, rooted up the beautiful vineyards in the neigh­bourhood of Aquileia, demolished the suburbs, and employed thetimber of the buildings in the engines and towers, with which onevery side he attacked the city. The walls, fallen to decay, duringthe security of a long peace, had been hastily repaired on this sud­den emergency; but the firmest defence of Aquileia consisted in theconstancy of the citizens; all ranks of whom, instead of beingdismayed, were animated by the extreme danger, and their know­ledge of the tyrant's unrelenting temper. Their courage wassupported and directed by Crispinus and Menophilus, two of thetwenty lieutenants of the senate, who, with a small body of regulartroops, had thrown themselves into the besieged place.
