Corsica, indeed, suf­fered a sudden convulsion from the teme­rity of the governor. The name of thisofficer was Decimus Pacarius. Thoughthe island, in a war carried on by suchpowerful adversaries, was of no impor­tance, he endeavoured to seduce the in­habitants to the interest of Vitellius. Theproject, which would have decided no­thing, ended in his own ruin. He sum­moned a council of the leading men, andcommunicated his design. Claudius Phir-ricus, who commanded the galleys on thatstation, and Quinctius Certus, a Romanknight, objected to the measure, and wereput to instant death. The rest of the as­sembly, terrified by this act of violence,swore fidelity to Vitellius. The populace,as usual, blind and ignorant, but by con­tagion catching the fears of others, fol­lowed the example of the leading chiefs.Pacarius began to muster his men, andtrain them to the use of arms.
