On a state fast, the 4th of February, 1740, our Authorpreached in Trinity Church on Prov. xiv. 34. " Righteous­ness exalteth a nation, but sin is a reproach to any people."This Sermon was printed at the request of his parishioners,and inscribed to them. The Right Hon. Edward Earl ofDerby had succeeded that nobleman who presented Mr. Smithto Trinity Church: but Mr. Smith still continued to be es­teemed at Knowsley, notwithstanding Knowsley had changedits master. He, who had been long considered as the Earlof Derby's Chaplain, was constituted in form, by letters patent,the 2d day of August, 1743. On the 31st of July, 1746, ourAuthor preached an Assize-Sermon at Lancaster, on St.John viii. 32. " Ye shall know the truth, and the truth shall" make you free." This Sermon is inscribed to the HighSheriff and Grand Jury, being " published at their command." In the year 1748, the Grammar-School of Brentwood, inthe parish of South Weald, in the county of Essex, being va­cant, was suffered by Lord and Lady Strange to lapse to theBishop of London, who, at their recommendation, appointedMr. Smith Schoolmaster there for life, by letters patent bear­ing date the 15th day of February, and by licence dated the17th of the same month.
