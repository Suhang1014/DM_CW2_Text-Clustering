Certain Thebans, fora very trifling offence, flew Clymenus during the cele­bration of the festival of Onchestian Neptune, this flightoffence having roused them to vehement wrath. Erginus,as being the eldest son of Clymenus, reigned after hisfather; and immediately as he came to the throne, havingwith the assistance of his brothers collected an army, ledhis forces against Thebes, vanquished the Thebans, andafterwards made a treaty of peace with them, On conditionthat they paid him an annual sum of money as a recom­pense for the murder of Clymenus. But when Herculesundertook to defend the Thebans, then the Thebans werefreed from this tribute, and the Minyæ suffered greatlyin war. Hence Erginus, who saw that his subjects werewearied in the extreme with the continuance of the war,made a peace with Hercules; and desirous that his king­dom might recover its pristine opulence and felicity, somuch neglected every thing else to accomplish this, thathe arrived at extreme old age without ever having been,married, or had any children.
