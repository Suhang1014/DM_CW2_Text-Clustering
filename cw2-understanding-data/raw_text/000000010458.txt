LXIX. On the following day, the de­puties from the senate, who according toorder attended at Ticinum, were admittedto an audience. That business over, Vitel-lius visited the camp, and, in a publicharangue, expressed a lively sense of thezeal which the soldiers had exerted in hisservice. This proceeding roused the jea­lousy of the auxiliaries. They saw theinsolence of the legionary soldiers, andthe impunity with which they commit­ted the most outrageous actions. It wasto prevent the consequences of this dan­gerous jealousy, that the Batavian co­horts had been ordered back to Germany,the fates even then preparing the seeds ofa foreign (a) and a civil war.
