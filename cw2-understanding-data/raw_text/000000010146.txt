THUCYDIDES, an Athenian, hath compiled the historyof the war between the Peloponnesians and the Athenians,as managed by each of the contending parties. He began towrite upon its first breaking out, from an expectation thatit would prove important, and the most deserving regard ofany that had ever happened. He grounded his conjectureon the earnestness of both the flourishing parties to make allnecessary preparations for it; and he saw that all the rest ofGreece was engaged on one side or the other, some joiningimmediately, and others intending soon to do it; for this wasthe greatest commotion that ever happened amongst the Gre足cians, since in it some Barbarians, and it may be said thegreatest part of mankind were concerned.
