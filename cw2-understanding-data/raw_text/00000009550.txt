When Nero had attained to such a distancefrom the enemy that his design might be disclosed withsafety, he addressed his soldiers in a few words, tell­ing them that 'no general had ever formed a designmore daring in appearance, and yet more safe in theexecution than his: that he was leading them to cer­tain victory; for as his colleague had not marchedagainst that enemy until the senate had given him sucha force, both of infantry and cavalry, as fully satisfiedhis utmost wishes, and those troops more numerousand better provided than if he were to go against Han­nibal himself, the addition thus made to it, whatevermight be its intrinsic weight, would certainly turn thescale in his favor. As soon as the foe should hear, in thefield of battle, (and he would take care that they shouldnot hear sooner,) that another consul, and anotherarmy had arrived, this single circumstance would in­sure success.
