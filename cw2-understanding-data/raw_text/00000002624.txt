After blackeningher reputation, and inflaming against her the passionsof the multitude, the tyrant accused and tried the em­press for a treasonable correspondence with the kingof Hungary. His own son, a youth of honour and hu­manity, avowed his abhorrence of this flagitious act,and three of the judges had the merit of preferringtheir consciences to their safety; but the obsequious tri­bunal, without requiring any proof, or hearing any de­fence, condemned the widow of Manuel; and her un­fortunate son subscribed the sentence of her death.Maria was strangled, her corpse was buried in the sea,and her memory was wounded by the insult most offen­sive to female vanity, a false and ugly representation ofher beauteous form. The fate of her son was not longdeferred: he was strangled with a bowstring, and thetyrant, insensible to pity or remorse, after surveying thebody of the innocent youth, struck it rudely with hisfoot:—Thy father (he cried) was a knave, thy mother awhore, and thyself a fool!
