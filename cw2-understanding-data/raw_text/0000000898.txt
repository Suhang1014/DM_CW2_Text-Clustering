Wealth and ho­nours, the offices of the state, and the ceremoniesof religion, were almost exclusively possessed by theformer; who preserving the purity of their bloodwith the most insulting jealousy, held their clientsin a condition of specious vassalage. But these di­stinctions, so incompatible with the spirit of a freepeople, were removed, after a long struggle, bythe persevering efforts of the Tribunes. The mostactive and successful of the Plebeians accumulatedwealth, aspired to honours, deserved triumphs, con­tracted alliances, and, after some generations, as­sumed the pride of ancient nobility.
