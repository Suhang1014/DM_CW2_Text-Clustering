When theyviewed with complacency the extent of their own mental powers,when they exercised the various faculties of memory, of fancy,and of judgment, in the most profound speculations, or the mostimportant labours, and when they reflected on the desire of fame,which transported them into future ages, far beyond the boundsof death and of the grave; they were unwilling to confound them­selves with the beasts of the field, or to suppose, that a beingsfor whose dignity they entertained the most sincere admiration,could be limited to a spot of earth, and to a few years of duration.With this favourable prepossession they summoned to their aid thescience, or rather the language, of Metaphysics.
