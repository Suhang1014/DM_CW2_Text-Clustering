It continues invisible, indeed, to the eyes of men, asdoes God himself; for certainly it is not itself seen while it isin the body; for it is there after an invisible manner, and,when it is freed from it, it is still not seen. It is this soulwhich hath one nature, and that an incorruptible one also ;but yet is it the cause of the change that is made in the body;for whatsoever it be which the soul touches, that lives andflourishes, and from whatsoever it is removed that withersaway and dies; such a degree is there in it of immortality.Let me produce the state of sleep as a most evident demon­stration of the truth of what I say; wherein souls, when thobody does not distract them, have the sweetest rest dependingon themselves, and conversing with God, by their alliance tohim ; they then go everywhere, and foretell many futuritiesbeforehand. And why are we afraid of death, while we arepleased with the rest we have in sleep ? and how absurd athing is it to pursue after liberty while we are alive, and yetto envy it to ourselves where it will be eternal ?
