It was also settled, that the houses should e*-no longer be contiguous, with slight party-walls to divide them (d); but every housewas to stand detached, surrounded and in­sulated by its own inclosure. These regu­lations, it must be admitted, were of publicutility, and added much to the embellish­ment of the new city. But still the oldplan of Rome was not without its advo­cates. It was thought more conducive tothe health (e) of the inhabitants. The nar­rowness of the streets and the elevation ofthe buildings served to exclude the rays ofthe sun; whereas the more open space,having neither shade nor shelter, left menexposed to the intense heat of the day.
