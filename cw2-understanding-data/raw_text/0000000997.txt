His lawful heir was driven into exile, theChristian priests were either murdered or expelledfrom their churches, the barbarous tribes of Albaniawere solicited to descend from their mountains; andtwo of the most powerful governors, usurping theensigns or the powers of royalty, implored the assist­ance of Sapor, and opened the gates of their citiesto the Persian garrisons. The Christian party, underthe guidance of the archbishop of Artaxata, the im­mediate successor of St. Gregory the Illuminator,had recourse to the piety of Constantius. After thetroubles had continued about three years, Antiochus,one of the officers of the household, executed withsuccess the Imperial commission of restoring Chos-roes, the son of Tiridates, to the throne of his fathers,of distributing honours and rewards among the faith­ful servants of the house of Arsaces, and of pro­claiming a general amnesty, which was accepted bythe greater part of the rebellious Satraps.
