The centurion,seeing that he had to do with a man, un­armed, indeed, but robust and vigorous,not a symptom of fear in his countenance,but, on the contrary, an eye that sparkledwith indignation, gave orders to his soldiersto seize their prisoner. Silanus stood onthe defensive : what man could do withouta weapon he bravely dared, struggling,and dealing his blows about him, till he fellby the sword of the centurion, like a gal­lant officer, receiving honourable wounds,and facing his enemy to the last.
