Here they made a descent, leaving a smallguard with the ships, made themselves masters of thesecantons, set fire to the houses, drove oft' a considerablebooty of men and cattle, and allured by the sweets ofplunder, proceeded still farther from the shore. Whennews of this was brought to Patavium, where the con­tiguity of the Gauls kept the inhabitants constantly inarms, they divided their young men into two bands,one of which was led towards the quarter where themarauders were said to be busy; the other by a dif­ferent route, to avoid meeting any of the pirates, to­wards the station of the ships, fifteen miles distantfrom the town.
