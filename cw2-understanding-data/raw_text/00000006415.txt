The Athenians seeming to acquiesce in these,at length, upon the third meeting, lest the smallnessof his own influence should be plainly detected, he de­manded leave " for the king to build a fleet, and to fail*' along the Athenian coasts, wherever, and with what-"ever force, he pleased." Here all accommodationwas over: The Athenians, concluding these points insu­perable, and that they were abused by Alcibiades,broke off in indignation, and return to Samos.
