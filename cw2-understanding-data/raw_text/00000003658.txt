The rescripts of the emperor,his grants and decrees, his edicts and pragmaticsanctions, were subscribed in purple ink, and trans­mitted to the provinces as general or special laws,which the magistrates were bound to execute, andthe people to obey. But as their number continuallymultiplied, the rule of obedience became each daymore doubtful and obscure, till the will of the sove­reign was fixed and ascertained in the Gregorian, theHermogenian, and the Theodosian codes.
