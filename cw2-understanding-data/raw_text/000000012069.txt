An absolute monarchy is a great misfor­tune for a nation, but it is a less misfortune than anabsolute oligarchy; and history cannot censure one whoimposes on a nation the lesser suffering instead of thegreater, least of all in the case of a nature so vehementlyearnest and so far aloof from all that is vulgar as was thatof Gaius Gracchus.
