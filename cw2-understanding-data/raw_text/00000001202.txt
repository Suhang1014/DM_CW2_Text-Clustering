In thecourse of these depredations, a great number of thechildren of the Goths^ who had been sold into cap­tivity, were restored to the embraces of their afflictedparents; but these tender interviews, which mighthave revived and cherished in their minds some sen­timents of humanity, tended only to stimulate theirnative fierceness by the desire of revenge. Theylistened, with eager attention, to the complaints oftheir captive children, who had suffered the mostcruel indignities from their masters; and the samecruelties, the same indignities, were severely retaliatedon the sons and daughters of the Romans*.
