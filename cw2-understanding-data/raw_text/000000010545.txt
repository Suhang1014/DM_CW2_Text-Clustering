His talentsand integrity gave offence to a lawlesscrew, with whom every virtue was a crime.They demanded his immediate execution.But their views were too apparent. Thebest and ablest men in Rome were doomedto destruction by a set of men, who pantedto let loose their rage, and lay a scene ofblood, of plunder, and devastation. Othowas not yet in fulness of power. His au­thority was sufficient to command the per­petration of crimes ; to prohibit them wasstill beyond him. The part he assumedwas that of a man enraged, and bent onsome atrocious deed. In that pretendedfury, he ordered Celsus to be loaded withirons, as a man reserved for heavier punish­ment, and by that stratagem saved himfrom destruction. XLVI.
