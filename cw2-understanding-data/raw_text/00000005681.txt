UN turning a little to the right hand from the straightroad of the Platxan land under Cithæron, you will fee theruins of Hysia and Erythræ. These were once cities ofthe Bœotians; and even now among the ruins of Hysiathere is a temple of Apollo, the half of which is entire,and a sacred well. According to the Bœotians, formerlythose that drank out of this well were endowed with thegift of prophecy. Proceeding from hence into the publicroad, you will again fee, on the right hand, that whichis called the sepulchre of Mardonius. Indeed that thedead body of Mardonius could no where be found afterthe battle in which he fell, is generally acknowledged;nor can they tell by whom he was buried. It appears,however, that Artontes, the son of Mardonius, gave greatgifts to Dionysophanes the Ephefian, and to others be­longing to the Ionians, that they might not be negligentwith respect to the interment of Mardonius. And thisroad leads from Eleutheræ to Platæa.
