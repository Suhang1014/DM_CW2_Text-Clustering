How manyRoman commanders might I name who never werebeaten ? In the annals of the magistrates and the re­cords we may run over whole pages of consuls anddictators, with whose bravery, and successes also, theRoman people never once had reason to he dissatisfied.And what renders them more deserving of admirationthan Alexander, or any king, is, that some of theseacted in the office of dictator, which lasted only ten, orit might be twenty days ; none in a charge of longerduration than the consulship of a year; their leviesobstructed by plebeian tribunes; often late in takingthe field ; recalled before the time to attend elections ;amidst the very busiest efforts of the campaign overtakenby the close of their official year: sometimes by therashness, sometimes the perverseness of a colleague,involved in difficulties or losses ; and finally succeed­ing to the unfortunate administration of a predecessor,with an army of raw or ill disciplined men.
