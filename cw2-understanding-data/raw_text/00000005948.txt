They instituted, however,many years after the Eleans, the pancratium among boys,the car drawn by two colts, and the vaulting horse: forthey instituted the pancratium in the sixty-first Pythiad,and in this the Theban Laidas was victorious: but onePythiad after this, they established the course with thevaulting colt; and in the sixty-ninth Pythiad, the cardrawn by two colts. And with the vaulting colt, indeed,the Larissæan Lycormas was victorious; but the Macedo­nian Ptolemy with the two-yoked car. For the Egyptiankings willingly suffered themselves to be called Macedo­nians, as indeed they were. But it appears to me, thatthe laurel crown was given as the reward of victory in thePythian games, for no other reason than because, accord­ing to report, Daphne the daughter os Ladon was belovedby Apollo.
