Scipio quickly conciliated the affections of hismen by his punctuality in discharging all arrears tothe guilty as well as to the innocent, and which wasstrengthened by the mildness of his discourse, and thebenignity of his countenance towards all without dis­tinction. Summoning an assembly on his departurefrom Carthage, after copious invectives against theperfidy of the petty princes then in rebellion, he de­clared that ' he was setting out to take vengeance fortheir crimes, with feelings very different from thosewhich he had lately experienced, while he was apply­ing a remedy to the error of his countrymen; thatthen he had, with grief and tears, as if cutting his ownbowels, expiated either the imprudence or the guilt ofeight thousand men by the death of thirty ; but now hewas proceeding with cheerfulness and confidence to thedestruction of the Illergetians: for these were neitherborn in the same land, nor connected with him by anybond of society ; and for the only connexion which hadsubsisted, that of good faith and friendship, they hadwickedly rent it asunder.
