The Syracusan generals, whose ardour other contin­gencies had already inflamed, and who had formed aresolution, even previous to such encouragement, tomarch their forces towards Catana, without the leastreserve gave implicit credit to this emissary; and,having instantly pitched upon a day for execution, dis­missed him. They also (for by this time the Selinun-tian and some other auxiliaries had joined them) issuedout their orders for the whole military strength of Syra­cuse to march out on the day appointed.
