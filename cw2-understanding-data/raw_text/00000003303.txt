Rome was now threatened by Astolphus; butamidst the tears of the citizens, the pope, Stephen theThird, embraced the generous resolution of visitingthe courts of Lombardy and France, to deprecatethe injustice of his enemy, or to excite the pity ofhis friend. The king of the Lombards was inexor­able; but Pepin, the son of Charles Martel, at thehead of a French army, accompanied the pope inhis return to Italy. The Lombards, after a weak re­sistance, obtained an ignominious peace, which wasviolated by Astolphus as soon as the French armywas withdrawn. Rome was again encompassed byhis arms, and again delivered by Pepin.
