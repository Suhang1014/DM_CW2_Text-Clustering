I think the English reader can want nothing more, to en­able him to read Thucydides with pleasure and profit ; es­pecially if he be at all acquainted with the Grecian history,of which few that ever read can now be ignorant, since Mr-Stanyan's History of Greece and the Universal History arein so many hands. I dismiss the work with some hope, butmore terror about its success. That hope is encouraged andsupported by the list of my Subscribers. There are Namesthat do me honour indeed; and which, whether the workmay suit the generality or not, will preserve me from everrepenting that I have bestowed so much time on translatingTHUCYDIDES.
