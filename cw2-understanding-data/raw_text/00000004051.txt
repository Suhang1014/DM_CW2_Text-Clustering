Now, at the same time that Titus Caesar lay at thesiege of Jerusalem, did Vespasian go on board a merchantship, and sailed from Alexandria to Rhodes, whence he sailedaway in ships with three rows of oars : and as he touched atseveral cities that lay in his road, he was joyfully receivedby them all, and so passed over from Ionia into Greece;whence he set sail from Corcyra to the promontory of Iapyx,whence he took his journey by land. But as for Titus, hemarched from that Caesarea which lay by the seaside, andcame to that which is named Csesarea Phillippi, and staidthere a considerable time, and exhibited all sorts of showsthere. And here a great number of the captives were destroyed,some being thrown to wild beasts, and others in multitudesforced to kill one another, as if they were their enemies.
