The next con­sideration was, in what place the adoptionshould be announced; in the forum beforean assembly of the people, in the senate,or in the camp. The latter was thoughtmost eligible: the army would feel the com­pliment; the affections of the soldiers,though of little value if purchased bybribery and low intrigue, are, notwith­standing, when they are gained by fairand honourable means, always of moment,and never to be neglected. Meanwhile,the populace rushing in crowds from everyquarter, surrounded the palace, burningwith impatience for the important news,and growing still more eager in propor­tion to the delay of the profound poli­ticians, who affected an air of mystery,when the secret had already transpired.
