The soldiers,who stood near his person, did not stay tohear the sentiments of the emperor, butbroke out with impatient ardour, exhort­ing him to summon up his best resolution.There were forces still in reserve, and, intheir prince's cause, they were ready tobrave every danger. In this declarationthere was no flattery: they spoke from theheart. In a fit of instinctive fury they de­sired to be led against the enemy; by theirexample the drooping spirit of their friendswould be once more excited to deeds ofvalour.' The men, who stood at a distance,stretched forth their hands in token oftheir assent, while such as gathered roundthe prince fell at his feet, and clasped hisknees.
